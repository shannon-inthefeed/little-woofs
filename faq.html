How many dogs do you walk at once? +
I keep all group walks to a strict maximum of 4 dogs. Small groups mean every dog gets proper attention, stays safe, and has a genuinely enjoyable walk — not just a chaotic outing. I'll never squeeze in an extra dog for the sake of it.
How long is a walk? +
Group walks are approximately 60 minutes. This includes travel time from collection to the walking area and back.

I use safe, familiar green spaces local to Thamesmead, Barking and Dagenham.

This depends entirely on your individual dog and their recall. I'll discuss this with you during the meet-and-greet and will never let a dog off-lead unless I'm confident it's safe to do so.

Yes — rain doesn't stop play! I walk in most weather conditions. However, in extreme heat I may adjust walk times to protect dogs from overheating, or shorten routes for brachycephalic breeds.

📋 Booking & getting started

How do I get started? +
Get in touch via WhatsApp (07810 350376), email (littlewoofs24@gmail.com), or the contact form on this website. I'll arrange a free meet-and-greet at your home, where we go through your dog's routine, health and behaviour. After that, if everyone's happy, we'll arrange a trial walk before regular bookings begin.
What is a meet-and-greet? +
A free, no-obligation visit to your home where I come to meet you and your dog. We'll go through a short questionnaire covering your dog's routine, diet, any health conditions, emergency contacts, and their behaviour around other dogs and people. There's absolutely no pressure — it's just a chance for everyone to get comfortable before committing to anything.
What happens after the meet-and-greet? +
If we're both happy, I'll arrange a trial walk — usually with just your dog or a very small group — before regular bookings begin. I'll also ask for a signed service agreement and arrange key handover for future collection.
✏️ Describe your exact process — do you have a printable service agreement yet?
Do you require vaccinations? +
Yes. For the safety of all dogs in my care, I ask that all dogs are up-to-date with their core vaccinations. I'll ask to see your dog's vaccination record during the meet-and-greet.

In the event of an injury, I will contact you immediately, administer basic first aid if needed, and take your dog to the nearest vet if required. Your emergency contact details are collected during the meet-and-greet for exactly this reason. LittleWoofs is fully insured to cover such circumstances.
