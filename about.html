About Andrea

From spreadsheets
to lead ropes

After years working in data analytics, redundancy could have felt like the end of something. For Andrea, it turned out to be the best beginning. Rather than diving straight back into job hunting — all the uncertainty, the waiting, the rejections — she decided to do something for herself instead.
✏️ Andrea — add your own voice here. What did you feel when you were made redundant? What made you decide to go for it?
A chance encounter at an airport, on her way to a holiday in Spain, changed everything. Andrea got talking to a man who'd been through something very similar — made redundant, unsure what was next — and discovered he was now a full-time, thriving dog walker. He handed over his card: "Let's connect after our holidays. I'll tell you everything I did."
✏️ You can add his name here if you'd like — or keep it as a lovely anonymous detail. Up to you!
She came back from holiday, made the call, got certified with the Modern Dog Walkers Association, and started building LittleWoofs — one dog at a time. She now walks five dogs regularly, all from her local community in Thamesmead.

What I believe in

The LittleWoofs way

These aren't just words. They're the standards Andrea holds herself to on every single walk.

🌿

Welfare first, always

Every decision is made with the dog's physical and emotional wellbeing in mind. Not convenience. Not cost. The dog, first.

🤝

Positive handling only

No harsh corrections, no punishment-based methods. Patient, kind, and encouraging — every time.

🔒

Safety and reliability

You'll always know when Andrea has collected and returned your dog. She doesn't cancel, doesn't cut walks short, and doesn't disappear.
